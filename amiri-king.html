Amiri King- Biography

Tony Donavan Schork, better known as Amiri King,  is a YouTube comedian best known for posting various videos on his channel RoyalMediaMafia, such as the “My Vlog Is Better Than Yours” video series, “Chevy Colorado Parody” and “Chevy Silverado Parody 2.”

What is the Net Worth of Amiri King? Salary, Earnings

His net worth is more than $150,000, which he’s amassed through his successful profession. His assets include a Chevrolet Traverse, a Cadillac, and a $285,000 property in Nicholasville, Kentucky.

Amiri King- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Amiri King was born on July 10, 1979, in Louisville, Kentucky, USA. When it comes to his childhood, Amiri was reared in Louisville by his single mother, Sharon Brown Colvin. In 1998, he changed his name to Amiri in honor of poets Countee Cullen and Amiri Baraka. Amiri had a tough life; at the age of 11, he ran away from home and spent some time living on the streets of his hometown. Later, as a 16-year-old, he was arrested and sentenced to three years in prison for first-degree armed robbery.

Amiri King- Relationship, Married Life

In terms of his personal life, Amiri is now rumored to be divorced from Sara King, with whom he has three daughters; information about their mother has not been made public.

Amiri King- Professional Career

Amiri began his career as a YouTube comedian in 2007 when he founded his official YouTube account RoyalMediaMafia, where he posted numerous videos, vlogs, and commercial parodies. However, the channel was suspended, and he then launched his self-titled channel, which he continued to produce videos on, beginning in 2012. Some of his most successful films include “Chevy Colorado Parody,” which has had over seven million views, and “Chevy Silverado Parody 2,” which has received over 17 million views, significantly increasing his net worth and boosting his notoriety. He has piqued the public’s interest with his “My Vlog Is Better Than Yours” video series, and he now has over 300,000 subscribers on his YouTube account.

In 2014, Amiri appeared in the independent film “Surviving The Zombie Apocalypse With Rick And Ed.” As a result of his achievements, he was named Leo Magazine’s Readers Choice Best Comedian twice, in 2014 and 2015, and received the YouTube Silver Play Button. He has also appeared three times on the cover of Readers Digest magazine, and he presently serves on the editorial and advisory boards of Tribunist.

Social Media Details

Amiri, a social media figure in the entertainment sector, is not only active on YouTube, but also on his official Instagram and Twitter accounts, where he has a large number of followers.
